# Contributing

Thanks for reading closely enough to want to improve this. *The AI Entrepreneur*
is written in the open, and a few kinds of contribution genuinely help.

## What gets merged

- **Errata.** Typos, broken formatting, dead internal links, factual slips, wrong
  dates, misattributed quotes. Small and correct is perfect.
- **Broken external links.** If a curated link 404s or has moved, a PR pointing it
  at the canonical replacement is very welcome.
- **Link suggestions.** A high-signal external resource a chapter's "Go deeper"
  section should point to — a canonical paper, a primary-source engineering blog,
  official docs. In the PR description, say which chapter, and one sentence on why
  it's better than what's there. Prefer canonical sources over aggregators.

Open a pull request against `main`. For anything larger than a one-line fix, a short
description of the change and its motivation makes review fast.

## What won't be merged

- **Rewrites of the prose.** The text is hand-abridged from the paid manuscript and
  is licensed CC BY-NC-ND (no derivatives). Style, voice, and structural edits to the
  chapter text are out of scope — please don't send them.
- **Promotional links.** Links to your product, course, newsletter, affiliate page,
  or SEO backlink target will be closed. "Go deeper" is for primary sources, not ads.
- **Scope-expanding additions.** New chapters, new sections, or content that belongs
  in the paid book rather than the free edition.
- **AI-generated bulk suggestions** submitted without having read the chapter.

## A note on the source of truth

The paid manuscript is the source of truth; the chapter files here are hand-abridged
from it after each editorial pass. That means accepted errata may take a beat to
reconcile with the book, and some "improvements" are declined only because they'd
diverge the two editions.

## Reporting without a PR

Not set up to open a PR? Open an issue describing the problem and where it is
(file and line). That's a real contribution too.
